About

I am an incoming tenure-track assistant professor in the Department of Biostatistics at Johns Hopkins University. The overarching goal of my research is to create targeted interventions to aid medically vulnerable and marginalized populations to prevent poor health outcomes and the social determinants of these outcomes. The unifying theme of my work is to address health inequities produced by structural injustice and improve fairness in algorithmic systems using detailed spatial and temporal observational data, modern methodology in causal inference and statistical machine learning, and cross-disciplinary domain expertise (more).

If you’re interested in my work or share interests, don’t hesitate to reach out. You can contact me at etchin at jhu.edu or follow me on Twitter and Mastodon.
