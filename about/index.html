About

I am an incoming tenture-track assistant professor in the Department of Biostatistics at Johns Hopkins University. The overarching goal of my research is to create targeted interventions to aid medically vulnerable and marginalized populations to prevent poor health outcomes and the social determinants of these outcomes. The unifying theme of my work is to address health inequities produced by structural injustice by leveraging detailed spatial and temporal observational data, modern methodology in causal inference and machine learning, and cross-disciplinary domain expertise.

I obtained my PhD in Biomedical Data Science from Stanford University, where I was advised by Euan Ashley and Trevor Hastie and my research was generously funded by the National Science Foundation Graduate Fellowship and Stanford Graduate Fellowship. Previously, I received my BS in Applied Mathematics at UCLA, where I worked with Xinshu (Grace) Xiao. I have also worked under the guidance of Rachel Martin, Carter Butts, and Pardis Sabeti, and as a machine learning scientist at Adobe Systems and Quora.
